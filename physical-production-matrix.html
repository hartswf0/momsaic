PHYSICAL PRODUCTION ACCOUNTCHINA vs USA · 2024 · what each territory actually makes

Not dollars — physical output. Each map colors provinces and states by how much they actually produced, in the commodity's own unit. One material system per row, each its own color. Darker means more. China leads steel, cement, cars, power, coal, and construction by wide margins; the two are close only in grain.

What this measures

This is not GDP. Every value is a physical quantity actually produced inside each province or state in 2024 — tonnes of steel, cement, coal, and grain; terawatt-hours of electricity. No prices, no dollars, no summing unlike things into one number.

Color is absolute output, shared between the two maps in each row. A given shade means the same quantity on the China map and the US map — so when the whole US map stays pale next to a dark China, that gap is the physical reality. Steel: China makes about 13× the US, and Hebei province alone out-produces the entire United States by ~2.7×.

Grain is the counter-case. The US Corn Belt — Iowa, Illinois, Nebraska — is a genuine giant; US grain runs about 75% of China's total. A territory can dominate one material system and depend in another. That's the point of keeping commodities separate instead of collapsing them into money.

Manufacturing and real estate widen the gap again. China built ~31 million vehicles to the US's ~11 million (about 3×), concentrated in Guangdong, Anhui, and Chongqing versus America's "Auto Alley" — Michigan, Kentucky, the Ohio-Indiana-Tennessee belt. On building floor space completed, China finished roughly 2.5× the US floor area even amid its property downturn.

Actual output only — annual production, not installed capacity or factory counts, which are different quantities and aren't mixed in. Alaska and Hawaii are omitted from the maps (negligible for these commodities).

Sources. Steel: China NBS/CEIC; USGS MCS 2024–25 & AISI. Electricity: China NBS/CEIC; US EIA.
